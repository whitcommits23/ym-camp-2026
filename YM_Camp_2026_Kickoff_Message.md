# Monday Night Kickoff Message
**YM Camp 2026 | Pioneer Trail Camp | ~4–5 minutes**

---

The pioneers walked 1,300 miles.

They didn't have a bus. They didn't have a schedule with free time built in. They didn't have WhirlyBall on Thursday. They had a handcart, a direction, and a decision to keep going when everything in them said stop.

And here's the thing about the pioneers that I think we miss. They weren't superhuman. They were scared. Some of them were your age. Some of them buried people they loved on the side of the trail and then got up the next morning and kept walking.

Not because it was easy. Because the thing they were walking toward was worth it.

---

That's what this camp is called. Pioneer Trail. And our theme this week is "Walk with Me."

I want you to think about that for a second. Not "follow me." Not "watch me." Walk with me.

That's an invitation. It means we're going together. It means nobody here is ahead of anybody else. It means the 12-year-old from Tallmadge and the 18-year-old from Youngstown are on the same trail.

We're walking it together. That's the whole point.

---

Here's what this week is going to ask of you.

It's going to ask you to show up. Not just be here — anybody can be here — but actually show up. Be present. Put the phone away during the firesides. Look at the person next to you like he matters. Because he does.

It's going to ask you to do hard things. Some of them physical. Some of them won't be. Being honest in a fireside is hard. Choosing to encourage someone instead of making a joke at his expense is hard. Deciding to be the kind of guy who leads instead of the kind who hangs back and waits — that's hard.

And it's going to ask you to let this week actually mean something. You can come to camp, eat the food, do the activities, go home, and forget it by July. That's an option. Or you can decide right now, tonight, that something changes this week. That you walk away from Friday different than you arrived on Monday.

I'm asking you to pick the second one.

---

The pioneers didn't know if they were going to make it. I'm serious — some of them genuinely didn't know. But they walked anyway. Because the thing they believed was bigger than the fear.

You're standing at the same trailhead tonight. Five days. Four themes. One invitation.

Walk with me.

Let's go.

---

*Delivery notes: Pause after the first line. Let it land. The middle section ("Here's what this week is going to ask of you") is where you make direct eye contact and slow down. The close should pick back up — end with energy, not a whisper.*
